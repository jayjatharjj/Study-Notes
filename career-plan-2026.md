## PART 1 — MARKET ANALYSIS (India, 2026)

### 1.1 Demand & hiring climate ✅
- **IT hiring is recovering**: +6% YoY in Feb 2026; broader white-collar market +12% YoY.
- **But recovery is concentrated UP the pay scale**: the 20+ LPA band grew **+19% YoY**, the 50+ LPA band **+45% YoY**, while **hiring below 20 LPA stayed flat**.
  - **Implication for you:** the sub-20 LPA band where most mid-level roles sit is the *most contested*. Your goal isn't just "switch jobs" — it's to break *into* the higher comp band, because that's where the demand growth actually is.
- Junior/mid hiring (0–3 yr) within IT is recovering at **+8% YoY** — the door is open at your level.
- **Market is bifurcated**: specialist shortage coexists with 100k+ traditional-IT layoffs in 2026 (e.g. TCS ~12,000 cuts). Generic Java-services profiles are squeezed; cloud-native + product profiles are pulled up.

### 1.2 The single biggest comp lever ✅
**Switching from IT services → product company / GCC** is the #1 salary multiplier at 2–3 YOE.
- Staying in services: ~₹4.5–7 LPA (2 yr) / ₹6–10 LPA (3 yr).
- Moving to product/GCC: ~₹14–22 LPA (2 yr) / **₹18–28 LPA (3 yr)**.
- **Realistic jump: 30–100%.** The "100%+ common" framing is the optimistic end — plan around **40–70%** as the realistic target, with 100% as a stretch if you land a strong product/GCC offer with a competing offer in hand.

### 1.3 Comp tier ladder (2–4 YOE, total CTC, metros) ✅
| Tier | CTC band |
|------|----------|
| IT services | ₹5–10 LPA |
| Mid-tier product / GCC / Indian unicorn | **₹12–25 LPA** ← your realistic target |
| FAANG / top product | ₹25–50 LPA (stretch) |

Your realistic achievable band: **₹14–25 LPA** at a product company or GCC, depending on interview performance and how well you position the cloud/AI work.

### 1.4 Where the hiring actually is — GCCs ✅
- **GCCs (Global Capability Centers) are the standout hiring engine**: projected **4.25–4.5 lakh new jobs in 2026**, growing ~20% YoY vs muted IT-services hiring. GCC share of IT hiring rose to ~27% (2025) from 15% (2024) — "adding jobs ~4x faster than IT services."
- **This is your highest-probability target tier.** GCCs value exactly your profile: cloud-native (AWS/Azure), microservices, and they hire heavily at 2–4 YOE.

### 1.5 Your skills vs. market demand ✅
- **Cloud-native talent deficit: 55–60%.** Your AWS + Azure hands-on experience and **Azure Developer Associate cert are genuine differentiators.** This is your strongest card.
- **GenAI talent shortage: ~90%** — BUT the strict definition means *model-building / fine-tuning / MLOps*, not LLM API integration. Your LLM-integration work is a **plus and a great talking point**, but don't position yourself as a "GenAI engineer" — you'd get caught out. Position it as "full-stack engineer who ships cloud-native + AI-integrated products."
- **Gaps to close before applying** (see prep plan): DSA fluency (product/GCC bar), system design depth, and being able to deep-dive Spring internals + your own resume projects under pressure.

### 1.6 Hiring seasonality & notice-period timing ⚠️ *(general knowledge — not verified this pass)*
- India hiring typically has two strong windows: **Jan–Mar** (new fiscal budgets) and **Aug–Oct** (post-H1, festive-season ramp before Diwali). **Jun–Jul** is moderate; **mid-Nov–Dec** slows (holidays, freezes).
- **Your 60-day notice** means: from accepted offer → joining is ~2 months. So an offer accepted in **late Sept/early Oct → join late Nov/Dec.**
- **Strategy:** finish core prep by late August, then **apply hardest mid-Aug through Sept** to ride the Aug–Oct window while you're sharp. Don't apply seriously before you're interview-ready — first impressions with a company are mostly one-shot.

### 1.7 Interview process by tier ⚠️ *(general pattern — not verified this pass)*
- **Service/mid-size:** 1–2 technical rounds (Java/Spring + some coding) + HR. Lowest bar, fastest cycle.
- **GCC / product mid-tier:** 1 online assessment (DSA) → 2–3 rounds (DSA + system design + Java/Spring deep-dive + sometimes frontend) → hiring manager + HR.
- **Top product / unicorn:** DSA-heavy (2 rounds) + system design + deep-dive + behavioral/bar-raiser. Highest bar.
- **Takeaway:** DSA + system design are the gatekeepers for the high-comp tier. That's where most of your prep time goes.
