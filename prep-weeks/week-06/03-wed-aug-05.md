# Week 6 · Day 3 — Wed Aug 05 — Referral Asks (Batch 1) + OA Drill

📌 **Today:** First wave of personalised referral asks (5–6, to engineers not HR) and your first timed OA-style drill so the real online assessment doesn't catch you cold.

**DSA maintenance (~45 min):** Two HashMap mediums:
- [ ] **LC 49 – Group Anagrams** (Medium) — HashMap keyed by sorted-string or char-count signature. Under 15 min.
- [ ] **LC 347 – Top K Frequent Elements** (Medium) — frequency map + min-heap of size k (O(n log k)), or bucket sort O(n). Implement the heap version. Under 20 min.

**Primary activity:**

*Referral outreach (~45 min) — Stage 2 asks, 5–6 today:*

A referred application is **~4–5× more likely** to get a callback. For each top target, find 1–2 engineers (SDE2 / Senior / Tech Lead — **not HR**) via LinkedIn → Company filter → "Java" OR "Spring Boot" → prioritise 2nd-degree and alumni.
- [ ] Send 5–6 asks. Be specific, make it easy to say yes, give them the material:
  > *"Hi [Name], thanks for connecting. [Company] has an opening for [role + req ID/link] that fits my background closely — cloud-native microservices on Azure/AWS, a 96% latency win, and LLM integration. I'm also available to join immediately. Would you be open to referring me? Happy to send my resume + a 3-line blurb you can paste. Totally understand if you'd rather not."*
- [ ] Rules: one message per person, under 150 words, a specific real detail about their company (not "I've always wanted to work here"), reference a specific open role. No resume in the first message unless they ask.
- [ ] Log every ask in the referral tracker: `Contact | Company | Connected | Asked | Responded? | Outcome`.

*OA / timed-coding drill (~45 min) — first rep:*
- [ ] Set a **45-min timer**. Two mediums, no hints, fresh editor — treat it as a real online assessment. Suggested: **LC 53 – Maximum Subarray** (Kadane's) + **LC 56 – Merge Intervals** (sort by start, merge).
- [ ] OA platforms (HackerRank/Codility/CodeSignal) often add: hidden test cases, strict time limits, sometimes MCQs on Java/SQL. Practise reading constraints first and handling edge cases (empty input, single element, overflow) — silent hidden-test failures are what sink OAs.
- [ ] After the timer: write time/space for both, note where you hesitated.

**Pipeline:** All 5–6 referral asks logged with a specific personalised detail each; OA result (time, both-pass?, weak spot) noted in your review card.

**EOD check:**
- [ ] Are all referral asks logged with a specific personalised detail each?
- [ ] Did you finish both OA problems inside 45 minutes?
