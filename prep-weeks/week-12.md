# Week 12 — Close / Buffer (Sep 14–20, 2026)

> The final week. Either you're closing the best offer, or this is your buffer to convert a late-stage loop. Because you can **join immediately**, acceptance → start is fast — use that to push fence-sitting companies to a decision. If the pipeline is thin, this week is a focused recovery sprint, not a panic.

> 📨 **Apps & referrals this week:** Accept the best offer by ~Sep 20–30; if the pipeline is thin, this is the fallback buffer — re-engage warm leads and nudge every late-stage loop. See the [cadence & tracker](applications-and-referrals.md).

---

## 🎯 Week Goal

1. **Close:** accept the strongest offer on the table, with comp evaluated on in-hand + guaranteed cash (see Week 10), and confirm a start date that leverages your immediate availability.
2. **Or convert:** if no offer yet, compress every late-stage loop to a decision this week and reopen the safety-net track.
3. Walk out with either a signed offer letter in hand, or a concrete, dated path to one.

---

## ✅ By Sunday you can...

- State which offer you're accepting and exactly why (numbers, growth, team), or name the 2–3 loops most likely to convert and your nudge plan for each.
- Confirm offer-letter details, start date, and the BGV document set without scrambling.
- Decline other offers professionally while keeping the door open.

---

## 📅 Daily Checklist (Mon–Sun)

---

### Monday, Sep 14 — Decision Matrix + Final Leverage Calls

📌 **Study today:** Build the offer decision matrix (in-hand · guaranteed cash · growth · team · stack) · run final leverage/nudge calls on every open loop

- [ ] Lay out every live offer/loop in one table: company, tier, **monthly in-hand**, **guaranteed annual cash**, variable/ESOP, designation, growth/scope, team signal, decision deadline.
- [ ] Rank on in-hand + guaranteed cash first (not headline CTC), then growth/scope.
- [ ] For every company still mid-loop: send a decision-forcing nudge — *"I'm close to a decision and [Company] is a top choice; is there any way to expedite the remaining step this week? I'm able to start immediately."*
- [ ] Confirm any verbal offer in writing before you treat it as real.

### Tuesday, Sep 15 — Negotiate the Lead Offer to Its Ceiling

📌 **Study today:** Final negotiation pass on the lead offer (base-first, then joining bonus/ESOP) · use competing offers + immediate availability as leverage

- [ ] Make one clean counter on the lead offer: a specific number (not a range), one reason, base salary first. Reference any competing offer factually.
- [ ] Ask for a joining bonus and/or ESOP sweetener if base is capped by band.
- [ ] Offer your immediate start as a concession in exchange for movement on comp — it has real value to a team under headcount pressure.
- [ ] Hold the silence after you counter; don't negotiate against yourself.

### Wednesday, Sep 16 — Accept + Offer-Letter Review

📌 **Study today:** Review and accept the offer letter (comp components, designation, start date, clawbacks) · get everything in writing

- [ ] Read the full offer letter: fixed/variable split, joining-bonus terms (clawback period?), ESOP vesting, designation/band, start date, and any conditions.
- [ ] Confirm the start date in writing — set it to leverage immediate availability, but leave yourself a short reset gap if you want one.
- [ ] Accept formally (email). Get a countersigned copy.

### Thursday, Sep 17 — BGV & Document Prep

📌 **Study today:** Assemble the background-verification document set so onboarding isn't delayed

- [ ] Organize one folder (digital + physical): prior offer/relieving/experience letters, last 3 salary slips, Form 16 / ITR, education certificates (10th/12th/degree + marksheets), PAN/Aadhaar, address proof.
- [ ] Since you've resigned, get your relieving + experience letter from your previous employer squared away now — it's the most common BGV blocker.
- [ ] Confirm the new company's BGV vendor and timeline.

### Friday, Sep 18 — Decline Gracefully + Close Loops

📌 **Study today:** Decline other offers professionally · keep relationships warm · thank referrers

- [ ] Decline remaining offers with a short, warm, specific note (you may want these companies later).
- [ ] Thank everyone who referred or helped — by name. This network is your next switch's head start.
- [ ] Update your tracker: mark final outcomes on every row.

### Saturday, Sep 19 — Fallback Sprint (only if no offer yet)

📌 **Study today:** If no offer: widen the net, re-engage warm leads, schedule remaining loops · otherwise light DSA maintenance to stay sharp until joining

- [ ] **If you have an offer:** rest. Optionally keep one DSA problem/day to stay sharp before joining.
- [ ] **If not:** reopen the safety-net track — apply to 8–12 more fast-moving roles, message every warm referral contact again, and confirm dates for any loop still alive. Re-read the funnel sanity numbers in [applications-and-referrals.md](applications-and-referrals.md) — if callback rate < 10%, fix the resume/keywords, not the volume.

### Sunday, Sep 20 — Reflect + Pre-Joining Plan

📌 **Study today:** Lock the outcome · sketch a 30-day pre-joining ramp (stack, domain, first-week goals) · or set the dated plan to close a pending offer

- [ ] If accepted: write a short pre-joining study list tailored to the new role's stack/domain so you contribute fast on day one.
- [ ] If still in flight: write down the exact remaining steps, owners, and dates for each live loop — and keep going. Offers in this market realistically cluster late-Sep → late-Oct; a slip into early October is normal, not failure.
