# Week 11 · Day 5 — Fri Sep 11 — Accept + Verify the Written Offer + Join-Date Negotiation

📌 **Today:** Verify every numeric field of the written offer letter against the verbal agreement, accept cleanly in writing, then negotiate the start date. Because you've resigned and can join immediately, the start-date conversation is yours to shape — **immediate availability is an asset, not a notice-period wind-down.**

**DSA maintenance (light/optional, ~15 min):**
- [ ] Optional — one easy LC only if a backup loop remains. Otherwise skip; you're closing now.

**Primary activity:**

**Pre-day check (5 min):**
- [ ] Open the tracker. Confirm the chosen offer and have the verbally-agreed numbers in front of you to check the letter against.

**Morning: Verify the Written Offer Letter (45 min):**
- [ ] Do NOT accept on a verbal number. Get the formal, signed, dated offer letter and verify every numeric field against what was agreed verbally:
  - [ ] Fixed base — exact figure.
  - [ ] Variable / bonus — target % and the conditions/payout basis.
  - [ ] Joining bonus — amount, and any clawback/retention clause (e.g., must stay 12 months).
  - [ ] ESOP / RSU / LTI — grant value, vesting schedule, cliff.
  - [ ] Designation / band — exactly as agreed.
  - [ ] Start date — as discussed.
  - [ ] Any conditions: BGV clearance, document submission, probation terms.
- [ ] If a number in the letter differs from the verbal agreement: flag it immediately and politely before signing — *"The letter shows ₹X for the joining bonus; we'd discussed ₹Y. Could you confirm and correct?"* Letters with "negotiable" or blank numeric fields are not acceptances — get every number filled and explicit.

**Midday: Accept Cleanly (30 min):**
- [ ] Accept in writing once everything checks out: *"Thank you — I'm delighted to accept. I've reviewed the offer letter and the terms match our discussion. I'm signing and returning it today. I'm genuinely excited to get started."*
- [ ] Acceptance is a commitment — once you sign, stop negotiating and stop shopping. Reneging after signing burns the bridge and the recruiter relationship.

**Afternoon: Join-Date Negotiation (45 min):**
- [ ] You've already resigned and can join immediately — reframe the classic "notice period" conversation as a **start-date negotiation where your availability is an asset, not a constraint.**
  - [ ] Lead with the value: *"I'm in a position to start immediately — there's no notice period to serve on my side, so I can begin as soon as your onboarding and BGV are ready."*
  - [ ] Use it to compress their onboarding timeline (start-date momentum keeps the offer from going stale) — but don't over-commit to a date before BGV clears; a documented BGV-contingent start protects you.
  - [ ] If you want a short personal buffer before starting, that's fine to ask for — frame it positively: *"I can start immediately; if helpful for a clean handoff of personal commitments, I'd propose [date ~1–2 weeks out]. Whatever works best for your onboarding."* You're choosing the date from a position of availability, not negotiating *down* a 60-day notice.
- [ ] Confirm the agreed start date in writing.

---

**Pipeline:** Once signed, mark the accepted offer in the tracker. Keep the other offers warm until tomorrow's graceful declines — don't decline before the signed letter is in hand.

**EOD check:**
- [ ] Did you verify every numeric field before signing?
- [ ] Is the start date confirmed in writing, with immediate availability framed as a plus?
- [ ] Did you stop negotiating and stop shopping once you signed?
