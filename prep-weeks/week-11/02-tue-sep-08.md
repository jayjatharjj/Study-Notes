# Week 11 · Day 2 — Tue Sep 08 — Competing-Offer Overlap + Timeline Compression

📌 **Today:** Engineer the offers to land in the same window so you choose from strength instead of being forced by whoever moves first. Run the competing-offer leverage call. Your **immediate availability** is an accelerant — it gives both sides a real reason to compress timelines.

**DSA maintenance (light/optional, ~15 min):**
- [ ] Optional — one easy LC only if a backup loop remains. Otherwise skip.

**Primary activity:**

**Pre-day check (5 min):**
- [ ] Open the tracker. For each live company, write its current stage and expected decision date.

**Morning: Engineer the Overlap (45 min):**
- [ ] The goal is to have multiple decisions land within the same week so you choose, not get forced by whoever moves first. For each live company, map: current stage → expected decision date → what's blocking it.
- [ ] For companies ahead of the pack, ask for a short extension on the decision window: *"I'm very interested and close to deciding. I'm also expecting to hear from one other company within [X days]. Could I have until [date] to give you a firm answer? I want to commit fully, not provisionally."*
- [ ] For companies behind the pack, accelerate them: *"I've received an offer I'm evaluating with a decision window of about a week — and since I can start immediately, timing matters on both sides. [Company] is genuinely my preference. Is there any way to compress the remaining steps?"*

**Midday: The Competing-Offer Leverage Call (45 min):**
- [ ] If you have two offers — or one offer + verbal interest — use the strongest lever at this band.
- [ ] Script: *"I want to be upfront because [Company] is my preference. I'm in the final stages with another company and expect a formal offer by [date] around ₹[number]. Before I decide, I wanted to give [Company] the first opportunity to move — and since I can start immediately, we could wrap up fast. Is there flexibility on [base / joining bonus]?"*
- [ ] If they ask to see the competing offer in writing: *"I'd rather not share a competitor's formal offer — that crosses a line in courtesy to them. But it's a real offer and the number is [₹X]."* You're never obligated to show it. Insisting as a hard requirement is a red flag.

---

**Pipeline:** Send at least one extension request and one acceleration request today. Log every recruiter response and updated decision date in the tracker.

**EOD check:**
- [ ] Are your live offers/decisions mapped to dates, with at least one extension or acceleration request sent?
- [ ] Can you deliver the competing-offer call in under 3 minutes, confidently?
- [ ] Did you frame immediate availability as the accelerant on at least one call?
