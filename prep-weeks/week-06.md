# Week 6 — Apply Wave 1 (Aug 3–9, 2026)

> Study is done. This is the week you stop sharpening the blade and start swinging it. Profiles go live, the first applications go out in real batches, and the first referral asks land. The realistic failure mode now isn't "not good enough technically" — it's "prepped hard, sent twelve applications, waited for inbound." This week kills that failure mode with volume, referrals, and a tracker you update every single day. DSA drops to maintenance — 1–2 problems a day to stay warm — and the primary work becomes pipeline: applications, ATS-tailoring, referral outreach, and timed coding-round drills so the first OA doesn't catch you cold.

> 📨 **Apps & referrals this week:** 15–20 applications (target GCC/product + 8–12 faster mid/services as safety net) + 10–12 referral asks. See the [cadence & tracker](applications-and-referrals.md).

---

## 🎯 Week Goal

1. Publish recruiter-optimised LinkedIn + Naukri + Instahyre + Cutshort profiles with open-to-work live, so inbound starts filling while you sleep.
2. Submit 15–20 quality applications in tracked batches — a curated GCC/product tier plus a faster mid-size/services safety net — every one ATS-tailored to the JD, every one logged.
3. Send 10–12 referral asks to engineers (not HR) at target companies, each personalised, each logged in the referral tracker.
4. Drill OA / timed-coding conditions until two mediums in 45 minutes feels routine — no hints, fresh editor, complexity stated.
5. Have a ready, non-anchoring answer to "available to join immediately?" and the "current/expected CTC" question before the first recruiter screen lands.

### Tuesday Aug 4 — ATS Tailoring Routine + Application Batch

📌 **Study today:** DSA maintenance (LC 217, 242) · the per-application ATS-tailoring routine · 4–5 more applications

**DSA Maintenance (30 min):**
- [ ] **LC 217 – Contains Duplicate** (Easy) — HashSet. Under 5 min.
- [ ] **LC 242 – Valid Anagram** (Easy) — frequency array. Under 8 min. State why a 26-int array beats a HashMap here.

**Core Block (45 min) — The ATS-Tailoring Routine (every application):**

Most applications in the sub-20 LPA band are filtered by an automated ATS *before* a human sees them. A resume that doesn't echo the JD's exact terms gets dropped silently. Make this a 60-second reflex:
- [ ] Keep a **base resume** + produce a tailored variant per JD in 60 seconds.
- [ ] Paste the JD next to your resume. Identify the **top ~5 skills/keywords** the JD repeats.
- [ ] Mirror them into your **summary + skills** section using the JD's *exact phrasing* — if the JD says "Spring Cloud Gateway," don't leave it as just "API gateway"; if it says "CI/CD," don't leave it as "DevOps."
- [ ] Verify each company's stack before applying — **don't apply to .NET shops.**
- [ ] Tailor the cover note / application message in 3 sentences: (1) specific relevant project, (2) tech match to the JD, (3) what you want — and drop **"available to join immediately"** in sentence 3. Never send the exact same note twice.

**Applications Block (90 min):**
- [ ] **Apply to 4–5 more**, every one through the routine above. Keep the GCC/product + safety-net mix.
- [ ] Running total target by end of Tuesday: **8–10 applications.**
- [ ] Log every row. Check inbound on Naukri/Instahyre/Cutshort and respond to anything matching your criteria within hours.

**First-Screen Readiness (15 min) — CTC + availability:**
- [ ] The "current/expected CTC" question hits in the *very first* recruiter screen. Have 2–3 lines memorised so you don't anchor low:
  - **Deflect (default):** *"I'd rather focus on the role and the total opportunity first — I'm confident we can find a number that works for both sides once I understand the scope."*
  - **Researched band if pressed:** *"For Java / Spring Boot engineers with my background in Pune/Bengaluru, the market is around ₹18–24 LPA — I'd want to be in that range."* Name the band, never your current number.
  - **Availability as a closer:** *"And I'm available to join immediately — no notice period to serve."* This is a real lever for teams with an urgent backfill; lead with it whenever timelines come up.

## 🧠 This Week's Operating Principles

### The volume + referral math (read your own funnel)
- **40–60 quality applications** by end of the application phase is the floor for generating enough loops to create competing-offer overlap. Week 6 is the opening wave: 15–20 now, more next week.
- A referred application is **~4–5× more likely** to get a callback than a cold one. Aim for a referral on at least half your target-tier applications.
- Rough funnel: 40–60 apps with ~half referred → **8–15 callbacks → 4–8 real loops → 1–3 offers.** Fewer than ~30 applications and the offer count often rounds to zero. That spread is *why volume matters*.
- If your callback rate after ~20 applications is **< 10%**, the problem is upstream — resume/ATS keywords, positioning, or too few referrals. Fix that before sending more.

### The two-tier application strategy
| Tier | Examples | Why | This week |
|---|---|---|---|
| Target (GCC / product) | Citi, Morgan Stanley, Walmart Global Tech, Razorpay, PhonePe, Atlassian | Higher comp, scale, culture — the real goal | Referral-first; 8–12 apps |
| Safety net (mid / services) | Established service cos, mid-size product | Faster interview reps, calibration, fallback offers | Cold-apply OK; 8–12 apps |

The safety net isn't a consolation prize — it's how you walk into the GCC loops already warmed up by real interview reps.

### "Available to join immediately" — surface it everywhere
Zero notice period is a genuine advantage in a contested band, especially for teams with an urgent backfill. Surface it in: the LinkedIn About + open-to-work setting, the notice-period field on every portal, the third sentence of every application note, every referral ask, and the first recruiter screen the moment timelines come up. It can move you up a queue and shorten a close — treat it as a feature, not a footnote.

### ATS routine in one line
Paste JD → mirror top ~5 keywords (exact phrasing) into summary + skills → verify the stack isn't .NET → 3-sentence tailored note ending in immediate availability → apply → log. Under 5 minutes.
