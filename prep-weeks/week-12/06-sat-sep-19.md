# Week 12 · Day 6 — Sat Sep 19 — Fallback Sprint (only if no offer yet)

📌 **Today:** A branch day. If you've signed an offer — rest, with optional light DSA to stay sharp before joining. If you haven't, this is your focused recovery sprint: widen the net, re-engage every warm lead, and lock dates for every loop still alive. Not panic — a sprint.

**Primary activity:**

**If you have an offer — rest + stay warm:**
- [ ] Rest. You earned it. The hard part is done.
- [ ] Optional: keep **one DSA problem/day** to stay sharp until your start date so day-one ramp isn't cold.

**If you do NOT have an offer yet — fallback sprint:**
- [ ] **Widen the net:** apply to **8–12 more fast-moving roles** (startups, recently-funded, actively-hiring teams — speed matters more than prestige right now).
- [ ] **Re-engage warm leads:** message **every** warm referral contact again — a brief, specific nudge, not a blast.
- [ ] **Lock dates:** confirm dates for any loop still alive; as an immediate joiner, offer to fit their schedule and start instantly.
- [ ] **Funnel sanity check:** re-read the funnel numbers in [applications-and-referrals.md](../applications-and-referrals.md). If your callback rate is **< 10%**, fix the **resume/keywords**, not the volume — more cold applies won't fix a conversion problem.

**Pipeline:** This day *is* the pipeline if you're in fallback mode. Log every new application, every re-engaged contact, and every confirmed loop date in the tracker.

**EOD check:**
- [ ] **If offer in hand:** did you actually rest (and optionally do one DSA problem)?
- [ ] **If no offer:** did you add 8–12 applications, re-message every warm lead, and lock dates on live loops?
- [ ] **If no offer:** did you check callback rate against the 10% line and fix the resume if it's low?
