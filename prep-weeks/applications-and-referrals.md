# Applications & Referrals — Quotas, Cadence & Tracker

> **Why this file exists.** Prep makes you *interview-ready*; it does not make you *apply* or *network* — and that's where the switch is actually won or lost. The realistic failure mode isn't "not good enough technically," it's "prepped hard, sent 12 applications, waited for inbound." This file makes volume + referrals a tracked, non-optional part of the plan.

## The targets

- **40–60 quality applications** by end of Week 11 (the floor for generating enough loops to create competing-offer overlap).
- **Referral-first:** a referred application is ~4–5× more likely to get a callback than a cold one. Aim for **a referral on at least half** your target-tier applications.
- **Follow up every application** that's gone quiet for 5+ days — once.
- Track everything. An application with no logged "next action" is a dropped lead.

## Weekly cadence (starts Week 5)

Applications stay **off** through Foundations (Weeks 1–4) — apply only when sharp. Weeks 5–7 are pure *pipeline-building* (list + warm connections). Real volume starts Week 8.

|------|-------|--------------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| 5 | Jul 20–26 | 0 — **build the tiered list** (~30–40 companies) | Connect with **10–15 engineers** at Tier-1/2 targets (no ask yet — just connect + a one-line note) | 0 |
| 6 | Jul 27–Aug 2 | 0 | Map **1–2 referral contacts per target company**; engage lightly (a genuine comment / congrats) | 0 |
| 7 | Aug 3–9 | 0 | Warm **5–8 relationships**; draft your referral-ask message | 0 |
| 8 | Aug 10–16 | **3–5 warm-ups** (services/mid-size — calibration reps) | First **3–5 referral asks** (on the warm-ups) | 3–5 |

| 10 | Aug 24–30 | **12–15** | Follow up **all** Week 9 apps; **8–10 new asks** | 27–35 |

**Rule:** scheduled interviews always override new-application volume in Weeks 11–13. Don't sabotage a live loop to hit a quota.

## Referral playbook — 3 stages

**Stage 1 — Connect (Weeks 5–7), no ask.** Find engineers (not HR) at target companies via LinkedIn "People" → filter by company + title (SDE/Senior Engineer). Prefer alumni of your college/companies, or 2nd-degree connections.
> *"Hi [Name] — I'm a full-stack Java/Spring engineer working on cloud-native + AI-integrated products. I've been following [Company]'s work on [specific thing] and would love to connect with engineers there. No ask — just building my network as I explore my next move."*

**Stage 2 — Ask (Weeks 8–10), once you've applied or are about to.** Be specific, make it easy to say yes, give them the material.
> *"Hi [Name], thanks for connecting. [Company] has an opening for [role + req ID/link] that fits my background closely — [1 line: cloud-native microservices on AWS/Azure, the 96% latency win, LLM integration]. Would you be open to referring me? Happy to send my resume + a 3-line blurb you can paste. Totally understand if you'd rather not."*

**Stage 3 — Follow up (Weeks 10–11), once, after ~5 days of silence.**
> *"Hi [Name], just following up on the [role] referral — no pressure at all. If now's not a good time, no worries; thanks either way!"*

(A longer recruiter/HM follow-up template lives in the Week 9 file — use that for post-application status checks.)

## ATS routine (every application)

Before submitting, **paste the JD into your resume's keyword check** and mirror the **top ~5 JD skills/keywords** into your summary + skills section (exact phrasing — "Spring Boot 3", "microservices", "AWS", "Kubernetes", "CI/CD"). This is what clears the automated screen, which matters most in the contested sub-20 LPA band. Keep a base resume + a 60-second per-application tailor.

## Sanity numbers (so you can read your own funnel)

- Cold applications convert to callbacks at low single-digit %; referrals at ~4–5×. With **40–60 apps and ~half referred**, expect roughly **8–15 callbacks → 4–8 real loops → 1–3 offers**. That spread is *why volume matters* — fewer than ~30 applications and the offer count often rounds to zero.
- If your callback rate after 20 applications is **< 10%**, the problem is upstream: resume/ATS keywords, positioning, or too few referrals — fix that before sending more.
