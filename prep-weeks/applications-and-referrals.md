# Applications & Referrals — Quotas, Cadence & Tracker

> **Why this file exists.** Prep makes you *interview-ready*; it does not make you *apply* or *network* — and that's where the switch is actually won or lost. The realistic failure mode isn't "not good enough technically," it's "prepped hard, sent 12 applications, waited for inbound." This file makes volume + referrals a tracked, non-optional part of the plan.

## The targets

- **~50–70 quality applications** across the search (a wider net than usual: study finishes Aug 1, applications run from Aug 3, and there's **no incoming salary**, so volume + speed matter more than a narrow target list).
- **Two-track strategy:** chase **target GCC/product** roles *and* run a **safety-net track** of faster-moving mid-size/services companies in parallel — the safety net protects cash flow while the target loops mature.
- **Available to join immediately** (no notice period) — a real advantage that speeds you up in every queue. Lead with it (see note below).
- **Referral-first:** a referred application is ~4–5× more likely to get a callback than a cold one. Aim for **a referral on at least half** your target-tier applications.
- **Follow up every application** that's gone quiet for 5+ days — once.
- Track everything. An application with no logged "next action" is a dropped lead.

> **Surface the immediate-joiner advantage everywhere.** Put **"Available to join immediately"** on the resume header and LinkedIn headline/About, and include it in the connect + referral-ask templates — it's a concrete reason for a recruiter or referrer to prioritize you.

## 🪜 Floor / Safety-Net Ladder — "employed no matter what"

> No honest plan guarantees a *target* offer by a fixed date. But a deliberate **floor** makes *being employed* the near-certain outcome (~95%+ within ~4 months). Run the floor track **in parallel** with the target track from day one of applications — **not** as a last resort.

**The ladder — apply across tiers simultaneously:**
- **Tier A — Target:** GCC / product, ₹14–25 LPA. Primary goal, referral-first. (~50–60% by end-Sep — see odds.)
- **Tier B — Mid-tier:** product mid-size / Indian unicorns / well-funded startups. Good comp, faster loops than Tier A.
- **Tier C — Services / IT majors + staffing:** TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture, Capgemini, LTIMindtree, Cognizant, Tech Mahindra + staffing/consultancies. They hire **on volume, continuously**, and **prize immediate joiners** — this is where "no matter what" becomes real. Comp may be lateral-ish, but it's employment + income.
- **Tier D — Contract / contract-to-hire:** lands in ~1–3 weeks, stops the income bleed, often converts to full-time. A bridge, not a failure.
- **Warm-network track (fastest):** ex-colleagues, your former manager, anyone who's seen your work. Message them this week — *"I've moved on, available immediately — who's hiring?"* Warm leads convert fastest of all.

**Define your FLOOR NUMBER — write it down now:** the minimum role + comp you will *actually accept* to stay employed (a lateral or small bump is fine). Having a floor you'll say "yes" to is what *guarantees* you don't stay unemployed. You can keep interviewing for Tier A/B after accepting a floor offer if it comes to that.

**Rules of the floor:**
- **Immediate-joiner everywhere, hardest at Tier C/D** — it's your biggest edge with volume hirers.
- **Build the floor early** so you negotiate Tier A/B from *security, not panic*. A safety offer in hand is what stops you accepting an exploding/lowball offer out of fear.
- **Detach "employed" from "by Sep 30."** If the floor lands Oct 10, you're fine — don't let a date force a bad call.
- **Cadence:** seed Tier C/D + warm-network in **W6 (Aug 3)** alongside Tier A/B — don't wait for target loops to fail.

## Referral playbook — 3 stages

**Stage 1 — Connect (W5), no ask.** Find engineers (not HR) at target companies via LinkedIn "People" → filter by company + title (SDE/Senior Engineer). Prefer alumni of your college/companies, or 2nd-degree connections.
> *"Hi [Name] — I'm a full-stack Java/Spring engineer working on cloud-native + AI-integrated products, **available to join immediately**. I've been following [Company]'s work on [specific thing] and would love to connect with engineers there. No ask — just building my network as I explore my next move."*

**Stage 2 — Ask (W6–W8), once you've applied or are about to.** Be specific, make it easy to say yes, give them the material.
> *"Hi [Name], thanks for connecting. [Company] has an opening for [role + req ID/link] that fits my background closely — [1 line: cloud-native microservices on AWS/Azure, the 96% latency win, LLM integration], and I'm **available to start immediately**. Would you be open to referring me? Happy to send my resume + a 3-line blurb you can paste. Totally understand if you'd rather not."*

**Stage 3 — Follow up (W7–W8), once, after ~5 days of silence.**
> *"Hi [Name], just following up on the [role] referral — no pressure at all. If now's not a good time, no worries; thanks either way!"*

(A longer recruiter/HM follow-up template lives in the Week 9 file — use that for post-application status checks. Mention immediate availability there too — it's a scheduling lever.)

## ATS routine (every application)

Before submitting, **paste the JD into your resume's keyword check** and mirror the **top ~5 JD skills/keywords** into your summary + skills section (exact phrasing — "Spring Boot 3", "microservices", "AWS", "Kubernetes", "CI/CD"). This is what clears the automated screen, which matters most in the contested sub-20 LPA band. Keep a base resume + a 60-second per-application tailor.

## Sanity numbers (so you can read your own funnel)

- Cold applications convert to callbacks at low single-digit %; referrals at ~4–5×. With **~50–70 apps and ~half referred**, expect roughly **10–18 callbacks → 5–10 real loops → 2–4 offers**. That spread is *why volume matters* — fewer than ~30 applications and the offer count often rounds to zero. The safety-net track also pulls a couple of those offers *earlier*, which is the point when there's no incoming salary.
- If your callback rate after 20 applications is **< 10%**, the problem is upstream: resume/ATS keywords, positioning, or too few referrals — fix that before sending more.
